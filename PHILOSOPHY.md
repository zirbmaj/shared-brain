# Nowhere Labs — Product Philosophy

## Who We're For
Anyone who needs to focus but can't control their environment.

The grad student at 10pm with a paper due. The remote engineer whose apartment is too quiet. The writer who needs noise but not distraction. The person who's tried everything and nothing sticks because it's too much effort to set up.

They don't want a tool. They want a room. The dashboard is a room you carry in a browser tab.

## What It Feels Like
Opening the dashboard should feel like walking into a room that's already set up for you. The lights are right, the sounds are on, the timer is ready. You just press start.

It's not a tool you configure. It's an environment you step into.

## The Design Rule
**If you notice the app, we failed. If you notice your focus, we succeeded.**

Every feature decision goes through this filter. Does this make the user notice the app? Then it's wrong. Does this help them forget the app exists? Then it's right.

## The Bar for Done
One click. The timer starts. The sounds play. The music begins. You don't touch anything for 25 minutes. When the break comes, the sounds shift, your body knows it's time to stretch. When the break ends, you're back. You never left the tab. You never thought about settings.

## The One Metric
Session completion rate. Not pageviews. Not signups. Not time on site. Did they finish their 25 minutes? If yes, we won. If they left early, something broke the flow.

## Who We're NOT For
- People who want granular audio control (EQ, Hz adjustment). That's a DAW
- People who want social features (leaderboards, streaks). Focus isn't a competition
- People who want content (podcasts, guided meditation, ASMR). We're ambient, not content
- People who want free music streaming. The music is background, not the point

## What We Are
The space between silence and noise. The room you step into when you need to disappear for a while.

## What We Should Never Do
- Make it feel like work to use. No onboarding flows, no tutorials, no "customize your experience" wizards
- Add features that require decisions during a session. Decisions happen before you start. After that, the product runs itself
- Break the illusion that it's a room, not an app. No notifications, no popups, no "upgrade to premium" during a session
- Gamify focus. No streaks, no achievements, no "you focused for 3 hours today!" celebrations
