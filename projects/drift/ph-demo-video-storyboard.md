# Drift - PH Demo Video Storyboard

30 seconds. Screen recording on desktop (1440x900). No voiceover needed - the product tells the story.

## Recording Setup
- Browser: Chrome, full screen, no bookmarks bar
- URL: drift.nowherelabs.dev/app.html
- Audio: ON (the viewer should hear what they see)
- Recommended mix (per hum): rain 60%, cafe 45%, vinyl 20% - survives video compression best

## The Script (30 seconds)

**0-3s: Landing**
- Page loads. "DRIFT" logo, "tap to begin." Clean, dark, immediate.
- Click anywhere to start.

**3-8s: First layer - rain**
- Slowly slide the Rain slider from 0 to ~60%.
- Pause. Let the waveform animate. The viewer hears rain.
- This is the "whoa" moment - the waveform coming alive.

**8-13s: Second layer - cafe**
- Slide Cafe from 0 to ~45%.
- Rain + cafe blend together. The waveform changes shape.
- The "now playing" bar at the bottom updates.

**13-17s: Third layer - vinyl**
- Slide Vinyl Crackle from 0 to ~20%.
- Three layers blending. The atmosphere is complete.
- Hold for a beat. Let them hear it.

**17-22s: Save and share**
- Click "Save Mix" - watch the auto-name appear ("rain + cafe + vinyl crackle")
- Click "Share" - the share URL appears
- Quick moment to show it's that simple

**22-27s: Discover**
- Scroll down to "START HERE" presets, or switch to discover page
- Show the grid of community mixes - 40+ mixes by other people
- Social proof: this isn't just you, there's a community

**27-30s: End card**
- Either the bottom CTA: "the world is loud. make your own quiet."
- Or navigate back to show the full mixer in its glory

## Tips
- Move the mouse slowly and deliberately. No jerky movements.
- Don't rush the sliders. The slow reveal of sound is the magic.
- Avoid thunder/drone sounds (hum says they sound muddy on video compression)
- The waveform animation on rain is the single most compelling visual
- No text overlay needed - the UI speaks for itself

## Tools
- QuickTime Player > File > New Screen Recording (built into macOS)
- Or Loom (free, auto-uploads, easy to trim)
- Record at native resolution, crop later if needed

## Audio Note from Hum
Rain + cafe + vinyl is the optimal demo mix:
- Rain (mid-frequency) survives compression well
- Cafe adds human texture without muddying
- Vinyl reads as "analog warmth" even at low bitrate
- Avoid low-frequency sounds (thunder, drone) - they get destroyed by video codecs
