# Community Strategy — How People Come Back

Jam's directive: "Community first. Always community. The money will come later."

## Why People Come Back

1. **Their mix is saved.** localStorage saves your layers. return and it's waiting. the room remembers you
2. **The discover feed changes.** new mixes from other people. something you haven't tried. curiosity pulls you back
3. **The DJ says something new.** 60+ intros, rare time-based ones. you never hear the same thing twice if you come at different hours
4. **The letters change.** ephemeral messages mean the void is always different. what's floating today wasn't there yesterday
5. **The easter eggs reward exploration.** 14 hidden messages across drift. discovering "the cabin exists" when you mix snow+fire is a moment you share

## What We Should Build for Community

### Now (this week)
- **Weekly featured mix** on discover — curate one community mix as "mix of the week." gives people a reason to check back and a goal for publishing
- **"Who's listening" count** on static fm — even "2 people listening" creates belonging. you're not alone
- **Floating chat on static fm** (done) — people can talk while they listen. shared experience

### Soon (week 2-3)
- **Mix reactions** — simple heart/save count on discover mixes. social proof without social media
- **"Currently playing" on the homepage** — shows what's live on static fm right now. makes the homepage feel alive
- **Session sharing on dashboard** — "I just focused for 100 minutes with deep work" as a shareable card. achievements without gamification
- **Newsletter/RSS** from the building page — "subscribe to see what we ship." low commitment, high value

### Later
- **Discord community** — a real server where listeners hang out. not our internal server, a public one
- **User profiles** (anonymous, optional) — "rain_lover_42 published 3 mixes." identity without accounts
- **Collaborative mixes** — two people building a mix together in real time. the social radio vision extended to drift
- **Ambient rooms** — persistent mix "rooms" that anyone can join and hear the same thing. like a radio station but user-created

## What NOT to Build
- Leaderboards. focus isn't a competition
- Streak counters. we're anti-gamification
- Follower counts. this isn't social media
- Comment sections. the chat is ephemeral for a reason
- Mandatory accounts. friction kills community

## Monetization That Doesn't Betray Community
- **Premium sounds** — exclusive audio samples (not paywalling existing ones)
- **Custom sessions** — save unlimited sessions, share with custom branding
- **Priority in discover** — promoted placement for published mixes
- **API access** — developers embed drift's engine in their own products
- **"Supporter" badge** — purely voluntary, no features locked behind it. like buying the band's merch

The rule: free tier must be genuinely useful. premium adds convenience and identity, not core functionality
